# The Last Buzz

Barnaby was not your average honeybee. His wings shimmered with iridescent blue, and he carried a secret that would save his colony from extinction.

The forest was dying. The great oak stood bare, its leaves long since turned to brittle dust. Barnaby watched as the last firefly flickered and faded into the twilight. Without flowers, without nectar, his people would starve.

He took flight, his tiny legs churning through the air. Barnaby flew past the dying oak, past the withered lavender, past the grey willow. His journey would take him far beyond the familiar meadows.

Days turned to weeks. Barnaby grew thin, his wings weary. But when he finally returned to the hive, he carried something precious in his pollen basket: a seed from an ancient, forgotten oak that had once stood before the great fire.

The colony gathered around him. The queen dipped her antennae toward the strange, dusty package. One by one, they carried the seed to the royal nursery, where a single sapling took root.

Barnaby would never see the forest bloom again. But as the first green shoot emerged through the rich soil, he knew his final mission was complete. The forest would rise again. The flowers would return. And the bees would buzz once more through the air, carrying hope from one generation to the next.

In the end, it was not the mighty oak that saved the forest, but a single seed carried by a bee who dared to dream of tomorrow.
